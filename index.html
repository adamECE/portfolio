About Me

My goal is to pursue a Software Engineering internship position in a company that is developing innovative technology and develop a dynamic skill set to tackle modern engineering challenges. I'm currently a Junior majoring in Software Engineering at the University of Arizona with a minor in Computer Science. I'm also a year-round Software Engineering intern at Raytheon Missiles and Defense, where I primarily work in embedded testing. Outside of work, I'm extremely involved on campus by being the Financial chair of the Japanese American Student Association. Outside of school and work, I enjoy cooking, chess, and learning about different cultures.

Raytheon Missiles and Defense is the world's leading developers in defense integration. As a Software Products Intern at Raytheon, I've developed a deeper understanding of embedded software and industry level development. My current tasks involve writing test scripts for telemetry data, running simulations, and performing reviews on embedded code. Development is primarily done using C++, MATLAB, and occasionally Python. During my time at Raytheon, I have also become familiar with the agile development methodology.
